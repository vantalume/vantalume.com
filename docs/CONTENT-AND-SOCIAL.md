# Twelve-month content and social acquisition plan

## Editorial system

Research the real customer question and current search landscape; create a brief with audience, decision, evidence and internal link; draft from primary sources and first-party expertise; obtain technical/expert review; fact-check every numerical or time-sensitive claim; create an original diagram or worksheet where useful; complete accessibility and metadata review; publish and distribute; add relevant internal links from existing pages; review performance and enquiries after 30/90 days; refresh material changes at least annually.

No article should be published solely to fill a keyword. A named owner must approve claims. AI may assist structure and editing but does not replace source checking or accountable review.

## Publishing sequence

- Months 1–3: the five published foundation articles plus items 6–15, two per month after launch.
- Months 4–6: product, mobile and performance cluster (16–30), three per month where expert review capacity allows.
- Months 7–9: answer-engine and responsible content cluster (31–40), two per month plus one original benchmark or survey.
- Months 10–12: trust, procurement and decision frameworks (41–50), two per month; consolidate pages that do not earn engagement.

## Instagram and TikTok strategy

Audience is business owners, operations leaders and product sponsors—not an audience of developers. Publish two useful short videos and one carousel/diagram weekly. Reuse each researched article as a video, carousel, LinkedIn note and email without copying trend formats.

Pillars: diagnose the digital problem; make/buy/stop decisions; short website teardowns with permission; workflow before/after demonstrations using synthetic data; calm AI risk explanations; founder build notes; scope and cost transparency.

### Scripts for the first 10 videos

1. **Need vs redesign:** “If your website feels old, pause. Can buyers understand what you do? Can they find evidence? Can your team publish? A visual refresh fixes none of those by itself. Name the business constraint first; then choose reposition, journey redesign, platform work—or a smaller repair.” CTA: save the redesign business-case checklist.
2. **20-second homepage test:** “Show the first screen to someone outside your company for twenty seconds. Hide it. Ask: what does this company do, who is it for, and what should you do next? Their uncertainty is more useful than your team’s typography debate.” CTA: try it and comment with the missing answer.
3. **Automation score:** “Before choosing an AI tool, score the workflow: frequency, time, exceptions, data quality and cost of a mistake. The best first automation is not the most annoying task. It is valuable, bounded, observable and reversible.” CTA: use the free Opportunity Mapper.
4. **Time-saving trap:** “An automation that saves ten minutes but creates fifteen minutes of checking is not efficient. Measure correction effort, exceptions, completion time and trust—not demo speed.” CTA: share with the person approving the pilot.
5. **Site or app:** “A website explains and converts. A web app performs an ongoing task behind an account. If the visitor only needs information and contact, do not fund a product platform. If they return to create, manage or collaborate, you may have an application.” CTA: map the journey.
6. **Smallest complete MVP:** “Minimum does not mean half a journey. A user still needs to enter, receive the core value, recover from ordinary failure and know what happens next. Remove use cases before you remove completeness.” CTA: read the MVP scope guide.
7. **Budget honesty:** “Under £5,000 can buy a valuable audit, prototype or tightly focused site. It usually cannot buy deep positioning, custom design, complex integration, migration and full assurance at once. Choose the decision that budget must unlock.” CTA: ask for a bounded starting route.
8. **Better enquiry form:** “A contact form should ask only what changes the response. Name, work email, organisation, need and a short description. Budget and timing can be optional. Every extra required field is a tax on someone asking for help.” CTA: inspect Vantalume’s progressive form.
9. **No email gate:** “We built a lead tool that gives the roadmap before asking for contact details. Why? Useful advice demonstrates judgement. A trapped PDF demonstrates a marketing automation account.” CTA: try the Opportunity Mapper.
10. **Human review:** “Human review is not proof that AI failed. In consequential workflows it may be the correct design. Decide which mistakes matter, who catches them and how work continues when the model is uncertain.” CTA: score one workflow this week.

## Measurement

Track qualified conversations influenced, Mapper completions, saves/shares from target roles, site visits from profile links and assisted enquiries. Do not optimise for follower count alone. Review monthly; stop formats that attract only peers without buyer questions after a fair six-post test.
